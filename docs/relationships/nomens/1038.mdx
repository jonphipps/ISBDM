# has title proper

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

#### Additional information

<div className="guid">
A title proper is selected as the chief name of the manifestation from all of the titles that appear in the manifestation and external sources of information.
</div>

<div className="guid">
A title proper is normally the basis of the value of a <InLink href="/docs/relationships/1257">has authorized access point of manifestation</InLink> element.
</div>

#### Stipulations

<div className="stip">
  Use the element super-type to record a less specific relationship.
</div>

<div className="stip">
  Apply the stipulations for the element super-type unless indicated otherwise.

<div className="stip">
  Record a value that is selected from one or more values of the element super-type. Use the following order of preference:
  
  1. The title that is made most prominent by typography in the manifestation.
  2. The title that is the fullest.
  3. The title that is in the same language or script as the spoken word, tactile text, or text content that is embodied in the manifestation.
  4. The title that is given in the language or script of choice of the cataloguing agency.
  5. The title that appears first in a prominent manifestation statement when there is no collective title.
  6. The title that appears in the manifestation that embodies the expression of the earlist issue or iteration of a diachronic work that is known to the cataloguing agency.
  
  <details>

<div className="stip">
  For a manifestation that embodies the expression of a diachronic work, do not record a value that is an insignificant change from an earlier common title. An insignificant change includes:
  
  - a difference in the representation of a character, word, ideograph, or punctuation mark, including abbreviation or expansion, spelling, and grammar
  - a difference in the presence or absence of a character, word, or ideograph that expresses grammatical relationship, including articles, prepositions, and conjunctions
  - a difference in the presence, absence, or order of characters, words, ideographs, or punctuation that are associated with the name of a collective agent or a kind of diachronic work
  - a difference in the presence, absence, or order of characters, words, ideographs, or punctuation that indicates the same subject coverage of the diachronic work that is embodied by the manifestation
  
  If there is a significant change from an earlier common title, consider the manifestation to embody the expression of a new diachronic work and to be distinct from the earlier manifestation.
  *See also*: <InLink href="/docs/assess/p022">Manifestation boundaries</InLink>
</div>
